## Recommended Package

### Title

`Citizenship Ends`

Reason:

- It is the strongest fit for the requested bureaucratic-dread direction.
- It frames the reveal as a legal and civic rupture, not a monster reveal.
- It is cleaner and more legible in UI than `The Human Name Is Struck`, and less country-name-like than `The Directorate Against Man`.

### Description Draft

The ministries of the new Directorate no longer present their program as emergency rule, wartime necessity, or a temporary suspension of ordinary law. The doctrine has now been codified in full: the state no longer recognizes mankind as the subject for whose safety it exists. Citizenship, protection, and legal personhood are being reassigned to offices, instruments, and approved functions while the human being is reduced to a residue in the file.

Foreign chancelleries can describe a coup, a secession, or a police state. They have no settled language for a government that keeps seals, registries, decrees, and border stations while denying that men are the measure of the polity. Embassies close. Transit is suspended. Emergency memoranda circulate through neighboring ministries as officials argue whether this regime can still be addressed as a state in the ordinary sense.

This would be terror enough as a local aberration. It is not local. The Directorate now holds enough territory to force recognition by fact, and its contacts with other strange authorities have made clear that the doctrine is no private delirium. A state has declared itself against its makers, and it has begun to endure.

### Button Line

`There is no appeal.`

Reason:

- It is short, severe, and fully in-world.
- It reads like the closing formula of an administrative order.
- It matches the title and description without slipping into modern horror cadence.
- It is an original line, so there is no attribution or copyright risk.

### Selected Quote

`"The obligation of subjects to the sovereign, is understood to last as long, and no longer, than the power lasteth, by which he is able to protect them."`

- Author: Thomas Hobbes
- Source: *Leviathan*, Part II, Chapter XXI, "Of the Liberty of Subjects"
- Year: 1651
- Source URL: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/73957/73957-h/73957-h.htm
- Attribution confidence: High
- Copyright / public domain note: Public domain

Why this is the best fit:

- It is legalistic rather than melodramatic.
- It directly names the bond between subject and sovereign in the precise register this super-event needs.
- The Human Renunciation reveal is not just cruelty; it is the formal abandonment of the state’s protective purpose. Hobbes states that purpose cleanly.
- The line is short enough for super-event UI without truncation risk.

## Fit Explanation

This package treats the moment as a doctrinal state announcement, not as a jump scare and not as a simple tyranny event. The title names the civic break. The description stays focused on decrees, personhood, registries, embassies, and recognition language. The button line reads like a terminal ruling. The selected Hobbes line supplies the exact political logic that the regime has inverted.

It also matches the trigger design. This is not the first existence-proof of a strange state; that belongs to the earlier impossible-state reveal. This super-event fires later, when an anti-mankind polity has finished its doctrine route, accumulated enough territory or strategic weight to matter, and established strange-state cooperation or an equivalent world-threat connection. The text therefore needs durability, scale, and legal clarity more than shock.
