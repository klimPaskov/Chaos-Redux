## Recommended Package

### Title

`The League of New States`

Reason:

- It is already the clearest title option in the prompt.
- It is formal and diplomatic rather than poetic or apocalyptic.
- It makes the formation legible at a glance as a bloc of newly independent countries, not a collapse afterimage.

### Description Draft

Several governments born from the Independence Wave have stopped behaving like isolated accidents on the map. Their delegates now sit under one charter, exchanging recognition, arbitration terms, and mutual guarantees while drafting a common language of survival. What began as scattered committees and emergency ministries has become a congress.

The new league is not powerful by the standards of the great empires, but it no longer speaks in a single frightened voice. Its members promise aid against coercion, submit border disputes to conference before war, and demand to be treated as states rather than temporary disorders. Foreign offices that expected these breakaways to beg for patrons now find a bloc at the table.

No capital mistakes this for a world crisis on its own. Yet the lesson is plain enough: weak countries have learned to negotiate together, and the map now contains a federation of necessity. The powers may dismiss it as fragile, but they must answer it as real.

### Button Line

`They sign because they must.`

Reason:

- Short and sober.
- Emphasizes necessity, not triumph.
- Fits a small-state defensive congress better than irony or bombast.
- Original line, so there is no copyright or attribution risk.

### Selected Quote

`"A general association of nations must be formed under specific covenants for the purpose of affording mutual guarantees of political independence and territorial integrity to great and small states alike."`

- Speaker: Woodrow Wilson
- Source: Address to a Joint Session of Congress on the Conditions of Peace ("The Fourteen Points")
- Date: January 8, 1918
- Source URL: https://avalon.law.yale.edu/20th_century/wilson14.asp
- Supporting source URL: https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/address-joint-session-congress-the-conditions-peace-the-fourteen-points
- Attribution confidence: High
- Copyright / public domain note: Historical U.S. presidential speech text from 1918; underlying text is public domain in the United States

Why this is the best fit:

- It directly joins the two essential ideas of this super-event: association and small-state security.
- It sounds diplomatic and foundational, not catastrophic.
- It reinforces that the league matters because it creates mutual guarantees, not because a major empire has shattered.
- It cleanly avoids Event 005 collapse framing.

## Fit Explanation

This package fits the First League role because it treats the moment as a formal diplomatic threshold. The title is explicit, the description centers congress, charter, recognition, and mutual guarantees, and the quote frames the league as a mechanism that protects both political independence and territorial integrity for small states.

Just as important, it does not read like Event 005. There is no Moscow, no ministry-collapse rhetoric, no roadblock imagery, no ledger-decay tone, and no suggestion that these states are debris from a single fallen imperial center. The emphasis is on coordination among newly independent governments that have learned to act together.

The package also respects the user correction on flags. The recommended text does not make flags the image, the argument, or the emotional core. The center of gravity is the table, the charter, and the diplomatic fact of bloc formation.
