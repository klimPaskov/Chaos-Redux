## Guards And Dampening

- Union Unmade first-month lock: `soviet_collapse_union_unmade_first_month_lock`.
- Union Unmade regular trigger ingredients: minimum breakaway count 5, high threat 60, critical authority 25, and sustained severe pressure alternatives. The breakaway ingredient no longer doubles as the calm/contested component-cap cutoff.
- Union Unmade terminal pacing tracks `soviet_collapse_months_active` and `soviet_collapse_terminal_pressure_months`. Standard terminal collapse now needs eight active months and six terminal-pressure months; war, Free Republics League formation, high chaos, or Kazakhstan failure can accelerate that to four active months and three terminal-pressure months; catastrophic authority loss, war/capital loss, or Chaos V can mature after two active months.
- Threat-ceiling recalculation, progressive-release checks, and UDC's Union Defense endgame route through `soviet_collapse_maybe_show_union_unmade_super_event`, so they use the same first-month, terminal-pacing, and severe-failure gates instead of calling Union Unmade directly.
- Dynamic component caps now run inside `soviet_collapse_recalculate_total_threat`. In calm strong-center conditions with no war, fewer than seven breakaways, and chaos below Rising Chaos, Republic Momentum is capped at 34, depot vulnerability at 34, Foreign Penetration at 18, League Cohesion at 18, and old-movement pressure at 12. That keeps repeated early pressure writes below the calm ceiling while Moscow Authority and Command Obedience remain strong.
- Contested-center caps remain looser but lower than the previous severe band: while Moscow Authority is at least 45, fewer than eight breakaways exist, and chaos is below Totalen Chaos, Republic Momentum is capped at 62, depot vulnerability at 60, Foreign Penetration at 48, League Cohesion at 52, and old-movement pressure at 45. Once authority breaks, war pressure escalates, high chaos arrives, or the breakaway count passes the contested cap gate, the caps no longer suppress terminal collapse.
- Component growth caps now also run inside total-threat recalculation. In calm strong-center months, Republic Momentum and depot vulnerability can rise at most 3 points from their last monthly checkpoint, Foreign Penetration at most 2, League Cohesion at most 2, and old-movement pressure at most 2. In moderate non-terminal months, those caps loosen to 6/5/4/5/4 for Republic Momentum, depot vulnerability, Foreign Penetration, League Cohesion, and old-movement pressure. This catches direct pressure writes that are not naturally routed through the foreign-pressure helper and stops Republic Momentum or Foreign Penetration from reaching 100 in a few days while the center is still strong.
- Terminal ordinary release includes Kazakhstan and vanilla-supported internal republic tags: `KAR`, `KOM`, `CRI`, `TAT`, `BSK`, `FER`, `YAK`, `BYA`, and `TAN`. The custom Far Eastern tag `FEV` is not in the ordinary/progressive release pool because it has no vanilla core-history surface; it remains reserved for its explicit high-chaos successor path.
- Event-created internal republic tags use `soviet_collapse_internal_republic_focus_tree` with legal, security, liaison, and tag-specific regional branch content instead of the generic fallback tree.
- Terminal collapse now runs league formation before anti-Soviet war entry. Local leagues auto-form only where two-member regional quorum exists, while Free Republics' League expansion skips countries already committed to a local compact.
- Monthly guard constants cap ordinary successful, calm failed, and moderate failed months at low deltas when the center is not yet genuinely broken.
- Foreign-pressure writes from Soviet objectives, foreign patron actions, regional faction failures, first-wave breakaway pressure, and focus foreign-channel helpers route through `soviet_collapse_apply_foreign_pressure_delta`, which tracks positive `soviet_collapse_monthly_foreign_pressure` and applies calm, moderate, and severe monthly caps before recalculating threat. Negative relief still lowers Foreign Penetration, but it no longer refunds the positive monthly pressure counter. Opening baseline writes still write directly by design and remain covered by component caps during recalculation.
- The 2026-05-23 focus-reward follow-up also expands direct custom-splinter and ancient-restoration `SOV = { add_to_variable = { soviet_collapse_* = ... } }` focus rewards into scoped blocks that immediately call `soviet_collapse_recalculate_total_threat`. This keeps route rewards that raise foreign appetite, old-movement pressure, depot vulnerability, military obedience, or related crisis components inside the same clamp/recompute surface instead of waiting for the next monthly or event-side recalculation.
- Monthly threat guard overflow is stored in `soviet_collapse_monthly_threat_dampening` and subtracted during later threat recalculations until a later guard pass no longer needs a cap. Timeout burst overflow raises that dampening only to the current over-cap amount instead of stacking repeated recalculation penalties. This closes the stale recalculation surface where the guard could cap `soviet_collapse_total_collapse_threat`, only for unchanged component values to rebuild the same threat total later in the month.
- Failed-objective timeout bursts now also run through `soviet_collapse_apply_timeout_burst_pressure_caps` during total-threat recalculation, before the Union Unmade check. Once multiple failures occur in the same monthly window, the helper caps total threat growth from the last monthly checkpoint in calm, moderate, war, Chaos IV, and Chaos V conditions. This preserves high-chaos escalation while preventing same-date timeout batches, including direct hidden timeout component writes, from outrunning terminal pacing.
- Soviet emergency mobilisation is limited to two reserve call-ups, uses a 63-day re-enable delay, spends stability, war support, and command power, and grants smaller reserve packets. Repeated call-ups add sharper authority, depot, and foreign-pressure strain instead of remaining a pure manpower/equipment faucet.
- Foreign patron eligibility now requires SOV to exist and the Soviet Collapse crisis to be active. Foreign aid routing no longer treats war with SOV or a bare coastal route as a completed physical aid route. Light diplomatic aid can use a faction channel, opened aid corridor, controlled shared border, or League conference channel. Heavy aid packages, including armaments, advisers, intelligence channels, volunteers, and construction missions, require either an opened aid corridor or a controlled shared border. Mutual coastal access remains only a potential route for opening the corridor, with the corridor decision's own cost trigger handling fuel, train, and convoy affordability.
- Foreign client dependency escalation is limited to client-style patrons. Protection treaties and adviser privileges stamp the sponsoring patron onto the target, so later adviser and client-cabinet steps require the same sponsor rather than whichever patron later becomes dominant.
- Moscow federal compact reintegration now removes the target from the breakaway array, decrements local/global breakaway counts, clears external-dependency flags, and leaves the target as a federated subject instead of a stale breakaway target.
- Manual Soviet objective activation has phase-band gates in the activation effect itself. This closes the source-level stale-queue surface where `activate_mission` could ignore normal mission activation and queue legal-settlement, late recovery, foreign, old-movement, regional, League, high-chaos, or cleanup objectives before the matching crisis phase exists. The activation helper prunes invalid active late objectives before counting the active-objective cap, stamps each pruned late mission's existing done flag before removal so it cannot return with a fresh timer, and resolved-breakaway/league-formation effects call the same prune when the crisis phase changes outside a mission tick.
- Mission success helpers do not raise the main threat total.
- Mission failure helpers are bounded by a verified maximum single-failure delta of 4.25 threat. With the active mission cap at 10, even a full first wave of maximum-pressure failures from the calm baseline reaches 49.75.
- Progressive release MTTH weighs threat, authority, command obedience, depot vulnerability, foreign penetration, League pressure, old movements, failed missions, war state, regional cascades, and chaos tier. The low/moderate/high/severe/critical thresholds are 35/50/65/80/92, `release_base = 0`, `miss_base = 200`, and `can_soviet_collapse_roll_progressive_release` prevents calm strong-center games from rolling extra republic releases merely because first-wave breakaways exist. Opening-lock component caps are tightened so contained first-month games do not carry inflated hidden republic, depot, foreign, League, or old-movement components after total threat is capped. Regional cascade roll pressure requires a real same-region cascade, such as two western republics, a local league quorum, or at least two smaller Central Asian republics, instead of treating ordinary first-wave spread as a cascade. The SOV scheduler stores last-month objective counts before the monthly guard reset, computes a hidden `soviet_collapse_release_urgency_score` from Republic Momentum, Moscow Authority deficit, total threat, Foreign Penetration, depot vulnerability, League cohesion, old-movement pressure, breakaway count, and last-month failures, and uses that score as the sustained-pressure gate. The random release weight starts from MTTH, then adds sustained accumulated pressure, active-region cascade pressure, and a scaled/capped share of urgency. The sustained-pressure accumulator now gains from urgency, candidate count, regional cascades, threat bands, weak center signals, and failed objectives, making repeated real pressure release republics more reliably without opening the calm first month. A high-threat plus critical-authority or critical-obedience gate prevents distributed collapse pressure from stalling solely because no single component crossed a release-level threshold. Cascade score can still reduce miss weight down to a fixed floor, while low-threat and strong-center miss modifiers can rise above `miss_base` up to `miss_weight_ceiling` before clamping. The roll gate treats current or last-month failed objectives as a release pressure signal, and the ordinary/progressive internal-republic candidate family uses vanilla `FER` rather than the no-core custom `FEV` tag.
- Timeout rewards that previously gave material, recognition, or League support to every breakaway from broad failure missions now route through `soviet_collapse_apply_timeout_*_to_random_breakaway` helpers for the main broad timeout set. `can_soviet_collapse_grant_timeout_aid` requires real release pressure plus either high total threat, prior/current failed-objective pressure, release-level component pressure, a true regional cascade, war, lost capital, or Chaos IV/V. A Soviet-side `soviet_collapse_monthly_timeout_aid_grants` budget caps timeout-aid recipients at two per monthly window, and each selected recipient still receives `soviet_collapse_timeout_aid_recent` for 30 days. The Iranian-road and Basmachi-supply timeout failures also use southern-only random-recipient helpers instead of arming every active southern breakaway.
- Foreign patron actions now stamp target-side timed flags. All foreign aid stamps `soviet_collapse_foreign_aid_recent`, heavy aid stamps `soviet_collapse_foreign_major_aid_recent`, volunteer aid also stamps `soviet_collapse_foreign_volunteers_recent`, and civilian/military construction also stamp `soviet_collapse_foreign_construction_recent`. This prevents a single breakaway from absorbing stacked recognition, materiel, corridor, League logistics, volunteer, and factory aid purely because the sponsor decision cooldown elapsed.
- The progressive release roll now belongs to the hidden 30-day SOV scheduler, not to every objective-board refresh or regional-league defensive-war effect. Those paths still apply pressure and recalculate threat, but they leave actual release rolls to `chaosx.nr5.129`. This preserves MTTH releases while preventing clustered mission completions, timeouts, or league war declarations from producing extra release rolls in the same month.
- Progressive release selection now uses `global.soviet_collapse_progressive_release_selection` as a scratch array instead of clearing the persistent first-wave memory array. Resolved Moscow-federated subjects are excluded from `is_soviet_collapse_progressive_release_candidate`, so MTTH pressure cannot spend a release on a republic already settled through the Moscow federal compact.

- `has_soviet_collapse_caucasus_league_quorum`
