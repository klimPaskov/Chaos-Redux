# Event 008 Stage IV Super-Event Research

## Scope

Event 008 `Tensions Rising`, first Stage IV firing of the `Diplomatic Fever` track.

Role: first Stage IV reveal / global escalation.

Constraints carried from the spec pack:

- non-terminal
- not a war-start declaration
- not a world-end scenario
- tone should center on lit ministries, sealed cables, public denials, quiet offices, and crisis psychology before open war

## Selected package

### Selected title

**The Red Line Disappears**

Reason:

- More specific to diplomatic collapse than the broader backup title.
- Suggests the vanishing of restraint, not the start of apocalypse.
- Fits Stage IV as the moment when every government behaves as if the limit still exists while acting as though it does not.

### Backup title

**The Whole Map Holds Its Breath**

Use only if implementation wants a more poetic and less diplomatic phrasing.

### Description direction

Keep the description quiet and administrative rather than martial. The strongest version should describe:

- ministries and embassies still lit after midnight
- sealed dispatches, copied denials, and staff officers waiting for orders no one wants to sign
- every capital insisting there is no crisis while acting as if a crisis has already been scheduled

It should not say war has begun. It should not claim the world is ending. The fear is that procedure, rumor, and readiness have outrun public language.

Suggested direction for the final localisation writer:

> The world still speaks in denials, memoranda, and sealed cables, but no one believes calm can be restored by phrasing alone. Offices remain lit after midnight, embassy gates stay watched, and every capital now behaves as if the first mistake belongs to someone else.

## Quote research

### Selected main quote

“the nature of War, consisteth not in actuall fighting; but in the known disposition thereto.”

- Author: Thomas Hobbes
- Source: *Leviathan*, Chapter 13
- Year: 1651
- Source URL: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/3207/3207-h/3207-h.htm
- Attribution confidence: High
- Copyright note: Public domain

Why this is the best fit:

- It directly describes war as a condition that exists before battle.
- That matches the exact Stage IV role: not open apocalypse, not the first battlefield, but the moment when the world is visibly arranged for conflict.
- The wording supports the super-event's office-at-night tone better than more famous war quotes that imply open hostilities have already started.

### Editing and conversion steps

Source inspection:

- confirmed source download from Wikimedia Commons redirect
- confirmed embedded metadata naming Mozart, Fulda Symphonic Orchestra, Simon Schindler, and the EFF Open Audio License
- detected `2.14871` seconds of leading silence and about `2.00392` seconds of trailing silence in the source

Final edit:

- trim start to musical onset at `00:02.149`
- retain `100` seconds of music for a final runtime of `01:40`
- apply only a short fade-out over the last `5` seconds
- no pitch shift, time stretch, or heavy mastering

Conversion command used:

```bash
ffmpeg -y -hide_banner \
  -i docs/super_events/source_audio/mozart_symphony_40_menuetto_fulda_original.ogg \
  -af "atrim=start=2.149:end=102.149,asetpts=N/SR/TB,afade=t=out:st=95:d=5" \
  -ar 44100 -c:a libvorbis -q:a 6 \
  music/super_event_008_red_line_disappears.ogg
```

Verification:

- final file probes at `44100` Hz
- final duration is `100.000000` seconds
