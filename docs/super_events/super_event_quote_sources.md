# Super-Event Quote Sources

This file records external quote sources used by Chaos Redux super-events when the source matters for completion proof.

## Fury

### Fury Becomes a State / The World in Fury

- Super-event IDs: `59`, `60`
- Localisation keys: `super_event.59.q`, `super_event.60.q`
- Quote in game: "The condition of man is a condition of war of every one against every one."
- Attribution: Thomas Hobbes, `Leviathan`, Chapter XIV.
- Verification source: <https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Leviathan_(1651)/Chapter_13> records the related Chapter XIII wording "such a warre, as is of every man, against every man"; <https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Leviathan_(1651)/Chapter_14> records the Chapter XIV wording used by the in-game quote.
- Use note: The localisation uses modernized spelling and a shortened excerpt. Keep the attribution to Hobbes, `Leviathan`, rather than a later quote collection.

## Holy Realm Buddhahood

### The Awakened One

- Super-event ID: `7`
- Localisation key: `super_event.7.q`
- Quote in game: "he stands, dispelling the hosts of Mara, like the sun that illuminates the sky."
- Attribution: `Vinaya Texts`, Mahavagga I.1.7.
- Verification source: <https://sacred-texts.com/bud/sbe13/sbe1312.htm> records the Mahavagga awakening verse in the same wording, with archaic diacritics in the public-domain Sacred Books of the East translation.
- Attribution confidence: High. The in-game excerpt is a shortened, modernized-diacritic line from the translated verse under the Bodhi tree.
- Use note: Keep this quote for the awakening super-event. It directly marks the moment after awakening and the defeat of Mara.

### Powers of the Awakened

- Super-event ID: `61`
- Localisation key: `super_event.61.q`
- Quote in game: "Get rid of this disposition, train yourself, and remain in that."
- Attribution: `Kevaddha Sutta`, DN 11.
- Verification source: <https://obo.genaud.net/dhamma-vinaya/pts/dn/dn.11.rhyt.pts.htm> records the Rhys Davids DN 11 translation in the same wording inside the "wonder of education" passage.
- Cross-check source: <https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/DN/DN11.html> renders the same passage as the "miracle of instruction" and supports the interpretation that instruction is superior to spectacle.
- Attribution confidence: High. The exact in-game wording follows the Rhys Davids line, while the cross-check confirms the same passage in a modern translation.
- Use note: This quote intentionally frames supernatural display as lesser than instruction, matching the super-event's first public miracle without making miracle the highest goal.

### The Final Silence, Non-Terminal

- Super-event ID: `8`
- Localisation key: `super_event.8.q`
- Quote in game: "Where is it that both name and form die out, leaving no trace behind?"
- Attribution: `Kevaddha Sutta`, DN 11.
- Verification source: <https://obo.genaud.net/dhamma-vinaya/pts/dn/dn.11.rhyt.pts.htm> records the exact "name and form" question and answer near the end of DN 11.
- Cross-check source: <https://www.accesstoinsight.org/tipitaka/dn/dn.11.0.than.html> translates the same passage as "name & form brought to an end" and gives translator/licensing context.
- Attribution confidence: High. The localisation uses a direct excerpt from Rhys Davids, shortened to fit the super-event quote space.
- Use note: Use this for the Empty Seat branch because the line emphasizes cessation without requiring the world-end branch.

### The Final Silence, Terminal

- Super-event ID: `9`
- Localisation key: `super_event.9.q`
- Quote in game: "As the extinction of a flame, even so was his mind's release."
- Attribution: `The Death of Buddha`, Mahaparinibbana Sutta excerpt in `Buddhist Scriptures`.
- Verification source: <https://sacred-texts.com/bud/busc/busc24.htm> records the same verse in the public-domain `Buddhist Scriptures` selection.
- Attribution confidence: High for the public-domain excerpt and its immediate verse context. Keep the visible attribution as `The Death of Buddha` because that is the source section title players can recognize, while this documentation records the Mahaparinibbana context.
- Use note: Use this only for the terminal Final Silence branch. The flame image fits the world-end silence without turning the event into a spectacle.
