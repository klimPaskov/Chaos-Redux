## Core expansion thesis
The duplicate clusters should be split into institution-specific payoffs. Each focus should answer a different player question: who controls officers, who trains the army, who speaks for villages, who protects minorities, who builds recognition evidence, who argues abroad, who coordinates allies, who hardens the map, who mediates host law, who musters host guards, who steals steel, and who weaponizes rivalry with the civilian builders.

Keep focus IDs and layout stable where possible. Replace duplicate `completion_reward` payloads with narrow scripted effects, flags, decisions, and AI modifiers that make the existing branches more reactive without expanding focus count.

## Accepted design constraints
- Do not rename focus IDs unless implementation finds an unavoidable localisation or asset conflict.
- Do not change tree layout, x/y coordinates, or prerequisite shape unless a replacement payoff requires a currently impossible downstream gate.
- Use existing script constants under `common/script_constants/005_soviet_collapse_constants.txt`; add new constants to the relevant category instead of hardcoding values.
- Use flags for boolean route state and variables for tunable progress or score.
- Prefer narrow new scripted effects over stacking more existing broad helpers.
- If a new decision is unlocked, put it in an existing category unless a focused category already exists and fits.
- Keep player-facing localisation as in-world text. Do not describe rewards as reworked or duplicate fixes.
- Register any new icons in `.gfx` and document placeholder DDS paths when implementation touches assets.
- Use `check_variable` for equality-or-threshold comparisons that need inclusive behavior; do not use `<=` or `>=`.
